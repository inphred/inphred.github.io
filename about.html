About InPHRED

Built on deep science.
Driven to deliver at scale.

InPHRED was founded to solve a 30-year unsolved problem in photonics — and bring the resulting technology to market across three high-growth applications.

Our mission
Redefining photonic performance — from fundamental physics to volume production.

The photonics industry has long been constrained by a fundamental limitation: the physical properties of InP and GaN materials make it nearly impossible to build homoepitaxial DBR mirrors with sufficient reflectivity for VCSELs and RC-LEDs.

InPHRED's founders, led by Yale Professor Jung Han, discovered that selective electrochemical etching of n⁺-doped semiconductor layers creates a nanoporous structure with precisely tunable refractive index — delivering the mirror contrast needed for lasing, using existing foundry equipment.

From this single platform, we are building manufacturable light sources across previously inaccessible wavelengths — for consumer sensing, digital health, and next-generation data center interconnects.

Science-first
We start with fundamental physics and build toward applications — ensuring our technology advantages are durable and defensible, not incremental.
Fabless efficiency
As a design house, we leverage the mature compound semiconductor ecosystem — combining premium performance with the cost structure of high-volume foundry manufacturing.
Platform thinking
One patented process applied across material systems, device types, and markets — every new application strengthens our IP position and scales our addressable opportunity.
Speed to market
By building on mature telecom InP and GaN epi, we eliminate years of development risk — our GTM timeline reflects real process maturity, not aspiration.

Company timeline

From lab discovery to commercial traction

InPHRED was founded in early 2024. In our first year we validated our core technology and established early commercial relationships across three markets.

Early 2024
InPHRED founded
Company formed around Prof. Jung Han's nanoporous DBR research, with initial focus on InP SWIR VCSEL commercialisation.
April 2024
$4M seed round closed
Seed funding secured to fund SWIR VCSEL prototype development, IP filing, and team formation.
Q2–Q3 2024
Platform expansion to GaN
Nanoporous process successfully applied to GaN material system, enabling visible RC-LEDs for health and USR interconnect applications.
Q3 2024
RC-LED delivered to Tier 1 OEM
Visible RC-LED samples delivered to a Tier 1 OEM partner for wearable health sensing qualification.
Q4 2024
SWIR VCSEL reliability certified
Industry-standard high-temperature and humidity reliability tests passed, validating nanoporous DBR longevity for commercial deployment.
Q4 2024
Tier 1 OEM engagement (AR/VR)
Custom SWIR VCSEL development engagement initiated with a leading consumer electronics OEM.
Q1 2025
Glucose sensor prototype demonstrated
First-of-kind non-invasive optical glucose sensor prototype demonstrated using SWIR VCSEL + multi-wavelength approach.
2025 →
Series B fundraise underway
Seeking $20M+ in Series B capital to scale manufacturing, expand the team by 2–3 key hires, and pursue all three market segments in parallel.
